High School

Unfortunately, computer programming is often left out of high school curricula. Here are some free resources to help students get started - and excel - on their own.

    Apps


    • Hopscotch

      iPhone & iPad App

      Ages 7-14

      Learn to code by making things you enjoy playing. Our award-winning block-based language was built from the ground up to be used on your touchscreen iOS device. With Hopscotch, you get a blank canvas for your imagination, a community filled with inspiration, and an audience to begin using your products the moment you publish them.

    • CodeMonkey

      Web App

      Ages 10+

      CodeMonkey is an online game that teaches programming in a real programming language called CoffeeScript. In the game, users control a monkey and help him catch bananas by writing lines of code.

    • Cargo-Bot

      iPad App

      Ages 10+

      Over one million people have enjoyed Cargo-Bot! The puzzle game that challenges your brain and helps you learn programming concepts.

    Programming skills are in high demand. Skilled high schoolers can get part time coding jobs making $30/hr. Some even start their own companies.

    Games & Competitions

    Everyone loves a good game. These games add an extra flare of compeition and excitement to practicing coding skills.


    • Hackathons

      Major League Hacking

      High School & College Students

      A hackathon is best described as an “invention marathon.” Anyone who has an interest in technology attends a hackathon to learn, build & share their creations over the course of a weekend in a relaxed and welcoming atmosphere. You don’t have to be a programmer and you certainly don’t have to be majoring in Computer Science. Hackathons are free to attend, are sponsored by some of the coolest tech companies, and have tons of prizes and free swag.

    • Scratch

      Massachusetts Institute of Technology

      Ages 8-16*

      Scratch is a programming language and an online community where children can program and share interactive media such as stories, games, and animation with people from all over the world. As children create with Scratch, they learn to think creatively, work collaboratively, and reason systematically. Scratch is designed and maintained by the Lifelong Kindergarten group at the MIT Media Lab.

    • CodeWars

      Programming Languages: Python, Java, Clojure, C++, JavaScript, Ruby, C#, Haskell, Swift, Elixir, PHP, TypeScript

      Ages 13+

      In our dojo, kata are real code challenges focused on improving skill and technique. Some train programming fundamentals, while others focus on complex problem solving. Each kata is crafted for and by the community.

    Programmers come in all shapes and sizes. In the tech world, age and location do not matter. Programmers are only judged by the coolness of their work.

    Courses

    Thousands of high schools do not offer computer science courses. The following free courses are available to everyone with access to the internet and can prepare students for internships in tech and freelance careers during or after high school, or provide a head start to collegiate computer science studies.


    • Khan Academy

      Programming Languages: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, SQL

      Ages 8+

      Learn the fundamentals of programming, explore programs made by others. Write your own programs and share them.



    • Codecademy

      Programming Languages: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, PHP, Python, Ruby

      Ages 13+

      Codecademy is an online interactive platform that offers free coding classes in 12 different programming languages including Python, Java, PHP, JavaScript, Ruby, SQL, and Sass, as well as markup languages HTML and CSS.



    • MIT OpenCourseware

      Massachusetts Institute of Technology

      Programming Languages: Python, Java, MATLAB, C, C++

      Ages 16+ (college-level)

      Through OCW, educators improve courses and curricula, making their schools more effective; students find additional resources to help them succeed; and independent learners enrich their lives and use the content to tackle some of our world’s most difficult challenges, including sustainable development, climate change, and cancer eradication.
